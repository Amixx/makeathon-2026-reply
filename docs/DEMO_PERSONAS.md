## Supporting Cast (the "also works for..." grid)

Shown for 10 seconds as slide 2 and again at close. Each persona demonstrates a distinct product capability.

### Jonas — the honest "I don't know yet"

- 1st-semester BSc Computer Science, TUM
- 19, from Nürnberg
- Picked CS because "it keeps options open"
- Vision-input answer: "I… honestly don't know yet. I like building stuff. I don't know what I want to work on."

*Product behavior:* Guided-discovery fallback kicks in. Instead of 5 cards, Jonas gets *3 exploration experiments* (one hackathon, one OSS project across a different domain every month, one professor office-hour to visit). The astronaut moment becomes the exploration.

*Why he's in the pitch:* Proves we work for the 70% of students who don't have a crystal-clear vision. Counters the judge objection "what if the user doesn't know what they want?"

### Aisha — the young professional pivot

- 27, PhD candidate in Climate Physics, TUM/Helmholtz
- 3rd year of PhD, 2 papers published
- Wants to transition to a climate-tech founder role post-PhD
- Vision-input answer: "I want to start or join a climate-tech company that does atmospheric monitoring at scale. Not sure when to make the jump."

*Product behavior:* Different agent weighting. Scholarship Hunter suggests EXIST-Forschungstransfer and Climate KIC. Career Agent surfaces climate-tech founders to message (via public founder communities). Extra Scout prioritizes CEU climate-tech accelerator events over hackathons.

*Why she's in the pitch:* Shows the product isn't "students only." This is the year-2 expansion market — young pros + PhDs pivoting. Answers "what's the TAM beyond undergrads?"

### Kim — the humanoid robotics dreamer

- 24, MSc Robotics, Cognition & Intelligence, TUM
- Wants to join an early-stage humanoid robotics startup (1X, Figure, Neura)
- Vision-input answer: "Humanoid robots in 5 years. I want to be building whole-body control stacks."

*Product behavior:* Career Agent notices her GitHub has no control-stack projects and suggests a specific OSS contribution to dm_control or mujoco. Extra Scout flags Neura Robotics Munich meetups. Scholarship Hunter surfaces DAAD research fellowships in control robotics labs.

*Why she's in the pitch:* Shows we track hot topics with specific data. Humanoid robotics is a 2026 zeitgeist moment — judges notice when we match the cultural current.

### Max — the non-engineer TUM student

- 22, BSc TUM School of Management, 4th semester
- Wants to work in EU AI policy, possibly at the Bundesnetzagentur or at the EU AI Office
- Vision-input answer: "I want to shape how AI actually gets regulated. Not lobbying — policy design."

*Product behavior:* University Navigator detects that TUM Management offers a "Digital Regulation" Vertiefung and flags the declaration deadline. Career Agent suggests the Stiftung Neue Verantwortung fellowship. Extra Scout surfaces Bayerischer Digitalgipfel, re:publica.

*Why he's in the pitch:* TUM is not a CS-only school. 40% of students are non-engineers. Proves the product scales across faculties. Answers "is this just for techies?"

## Why Lina Beats the Alternatives (recap for anyone reading this doc cold)

- *Not a literal astronaut* — becoming one is a 20-year military/ESA-selection path the product genuinely cannot help with. Judges would discount.
- *Not "becoming a CTO"* — title-as-goal is weak narrative and emotionally flat.
- *Not a generic TUM student* — too unspecific to produce believable agent outputs in the demo.
- *Lina is the sweet spot:* ambitious enough to resonate with the astronaut metaphor, plausible enough for judges to believe, and specific enough that every agent surfaces a real, verifiable, clickable output.

She's the moonshot. She's also every other smart, uncertain TUM student who looks at their peers and thinks "am I actually good enough for this?" That recognition is the pitch.
